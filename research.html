Research

Methodology, models, and structural analysis.

The Unfound Project examines missing persons investigations as institutional systems shaped by classification, response speed, preservation, visibility, and long-term investigative persistence.

Research Overview

A structural approach to disappearance investigations

The Unfound Project applies systems analysis to missing persons response in the United States. Rather than focusing primarily on narrative reconstruction, the project studies the institutional architecture that shapes investigative trajectory over time.

This includes classification decisions, response speed, evidence preservation, search conditions, media amplification, inter-agency coordination, and the long-term elasticity of unresolved cases.

The objective is not to sensationalize disappearance. It is to clarify why some cases sustain momentum while others narrow rapidly under conditions of ambiguity, fragmentation, and structural constraint.

Methodology

How the research is structured

The project combines analytical modeling, publicly documented case review, and institutional process analysis to understand divergence in investigative outcomes.

Trajectory Modeling

Cases are examined as evolving trajectories shaped by identifiable structural variables rather than as isolated narrative mysteries.

Evidence Preservation Analysis

The project evaluates how timing influences the survival of digital, testimonial, and physical evidence.

Search Probability Frameworks

Search conditions are considered through terrain, uncertainty, last known point, and the expansion of possible movement fields over time.

Media Amplification Dynamics

Public visibility is studied as a structural variable that can expand awareness, generate leads, and alter investigative elasticity.

Institutional Coordination Analysis

The project evaluates how fragmented systems, jurisdictional boundaries, and uneven reporting standards influence continuity and clarity.

Policy-Oriented Inquiry

The research identifies leverage points where procedural reform may reduce preventable evidence loss and investigative narrowing.

Core Model

The Investigative Trajectory Model

The central framework used by The Unfound Project is the Investigative Trajectory Model. It describes how investigative outcomes are shaped by a chain of structural variables extending from initial classification through long-term resolution probability.
