About the Book

Every year, hundreds of thousands of missing persons reports are filed in the United States. Yet investigative outcomes diverge sharply. Some cases generate rapid coordination and sustained attention. Others stall within hours shaped by classification decisions, jurisdictional boundaries, data fragmentation, and media silence.

Unfound America examines the structural architecture behind those outcomes.

Rather than retelling individual disappearances, the book analyzes the systems that determine investigative trajectory: how initial response speed narrows evidence windows, how reporting frameworks influence visibility, how inter-agency coordination affects momentum, and how public amplification reshapes investigative elasticity over time.

Project Premise

Missing persons investigations do not diverge at random.

Some cases generate rapid coordination, sustained public attention, and durable investigative momentum. Others narrow quickly. The Unfound Project focuses on the structural variables that shape those outcomes, treating disappearance not as isolated mystery alone, but as a process influenced by institutional design.
