What this project is

A serious investigative research platform

The Unfound Project is an independent research and analysis initiative focused on institutional architecture rather than narrative true crime storytelling. It sits at the intersection of criminology, investigative journalism, public policy, sociology of institutions, and media systems analysis.

Research

Structural analysis

Examination of classification systems, search probability, evidence preservation windows, jurisdictional fragmentation, and digital visibility dynamics.

Archive

Case micro-studies

Cases are used as structural illustrations rather than retold as sensational narrative. Each page isolates variables, constraints, and takeaways.

Public work

Book, essays, and media

The ecosystem includes the book Unfound America, research essays, and case analysis intended to support more informed public discussion.

The Book

Unfound America

Unfound America: The Architecture of Missing Persons Response examines how institutional design influences missing persons investigations through systems analysis, case micro-studies, and policy discussion.

The manuscript focuses on classification systems, the first 48 hours, search probability, data fragmentation, digital evidence preservation, media amplification, adult alert systems, cold case infrastructure, and responsible amplification.

Research Areas

Where the analysis concentrates

01

Classification and urgency

How administrative framing influences escalation thresholds, investigative posture, and early public visibility.

02

Evidence preservation windows

How the speed of digital and physical evidence preservation affects clarity, optionality, and long-term momentum.

03

Data fragmentation

How decentralized reporting systems and uneven participation alter national visibility and pattern recognition.

04

Media amplification dynamics

How visibility inequality, engagement incentives, and algorithmic systems redistribute public attention.

05

Search science and terrain

How probability-based search planning, geography, and evidence density shape divergence from the start.

06

Cold case persistence

How infrastructure, preservation, and periodic reactivation affect unresolved cases over the long term.
