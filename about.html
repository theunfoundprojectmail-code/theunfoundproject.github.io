About

A restrained analytical project

The Unfound Project is an independent research and analysis initiative examining the structural mechanics of missing persons investigations.

Purpose

The project studies how systems, policies, and information flows influence the trajectory and resolution probability of disappearance investigations.

It is not designed as sensational true crime content. It is designed as a serious investigative research platform.

Disciplines

  • Criminology
  • Investigative journalism
  • Public policy
  • Sociology of institutions
  • Media systems analysis

Tone

Calm. Measured. Fact-based. Structural. Policy-oriented.

Human elements may appear briefly for grounding, but they do not dominate the analysis.

Long-term vision

The broader ecosystem includes the book, a research website, a growing case archive, analytical essays, and media work that brings structural thinking into public discussion.
