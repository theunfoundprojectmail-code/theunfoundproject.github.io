About

The Unfound Project

The Unfound Project is an independent research initiative examining the structural architecture of missing persons investigations.

Each year, hundreds of thousands of missing persons reports are filed in the United States. While many cases resolve quickly, others diverge dramatically in investigative outcome. Some generate immediate coordination and sustained visibility. Others narrow rapidly under conditions of uncertainty, fragmented information, and limited institutional response.

The Unfound Project studies the systems that shape those outcomes.

Research Focus

Investigative Structure

Rather than treating disappearances primarily as narrative mysteries, the project examines the institutional variables that influence investigative trajectory. These include classification decisions, response speed, evidence preservation, investigative clarity, media amplification, and long-term investigative persistence.

Understanding these mechanisms helps explain why some cases retain momentum while others lose investigative visibility over time.

The Framework

The Investigative Trajectory Model

The analytical framework used throughout the project is the Investigative Trajectory Model. It describes how investigative outcomes evolve through a sequence of structural variables beginning at the moment of disappearance and extending through long-term investigative persistence.

Disappearance Event → Classification Decision → Response Speed → Evidence Preservation → Investigative Clarity → Public Amplification → Investigative Elasticity → Resolution Probability

This model serves as the intellectual foundation for the project's research, case archive, and book.

The Book

Unfound America

The project's central publication, Unfound America: The Architecture of Missing Persons Response, expands this framework through structural analysis chapters, investigative process explanations, and case micro-studies. The book examines how institutional design influences investigative probability across time.

Rather than focusing solely on individual disappearance narratives, the work investigates the systems that determine whether investigative momentum expands, narrows, or stalls.

Editorial Approach

Responsible Analysis

The Unfound Project prioritizes careful, fact-based analysis. Case discussions rely on publicly documented reporting, verified timelines, and institutional process explanations. The project avoids speculation and sensational presentation in order to maintain focus on structural mechanisms.

Missing persons investigations involve real individuals and families. The purpose of this work is not entertainment, but understanding how investigative systems function and where improvements may increase investigative probability.

Purpose

Why This Work Exists

Disappearance itself may be unpredictable. The response architecture that follows is not.

By examining how investigative systems allocate attention, preserve evidence, and sustain visibility, The Unfound Project seeks to clarify the structural forces that shape what is found, what is lost, and what remains unresolved.
